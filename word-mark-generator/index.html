Important guidelines

These are wordmarks, not logos. A wordmark is a text-based identifier that may include an icon — it is not a replacement for the OCHA logo.

  • All wordmarks require approval by the OCHA Brand and Design Unit.
  • Use wordmarks only when strictly necessary to identify a specific programme, project, or initiative.
  • Always prioritise the OCHA logo to maintain organisational visibility and brand recognition.
  • Avoid creating wordmarks for every product, event, or output — overuse weakens the OCHA brand.
  • When in doubt, use the OCHA logo instead.
  • Not all Humanitarian Icons are available as wordmarks. The selection is based on relevance and design suitability. If you need an icon that is not listed, contact us to discuss adding it.

For guidance on branding and visual identity, visit brand.unocha.org or contact ochavisual@un.org.
